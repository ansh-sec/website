## Why coordination matters

Most children with developmental needs don't require just one kind of support. They need speech, occupational, and behavioral therapy working together. Yet in fragmented systems, each therapist works in a silo — different goals, different reports, different rhythms.

**Guided Growth is our answer to that fragmentation.**

## The problem with fragmented care

When therapy isn't coordinated:

- Goals conflict or overlap inefficiently
- Parents repeat the same intake stories to each new therapist
- Progress is uneven across disciplines
- Scheduling and costs spiral
- The child experiences therapy as disconnected pieces

## What coordinated care looks like

In Guided Growth:

1. **One lead clinician** owns the overall plan
2. **One intake** informs every discipline
3. **Shared goals** across therapies, so the child isn't juggling three separate agendas
4. **Monthly case conferences** where the team aligns
5. **One progress report** for the family — clear, honest, and cohesive

## Who benefits most

Guided Growth is designed for children who would benefit from **two or more disciplines** over a sustained period — typically 12 months. Common profiles include:

- Children on the autism spectrum with speech, sensory, and behavioral needs
- Children with global developmental delay
- Children with complex learning and attention profiles

## What outcomes parents can expect

At 12 months, families typically report:

- Measurable progress across every targeted domain
- Greater parent confidence in day-to-day support
- A clear transition plan into school, maintenance therapy, or next-phase care
- Reduced family stress

## A note on commitment

Twelve months is a meaningful commitment. But for many families, it's the difference between *therapy that helps a little* and *therapy that transforms daily life*.
