## Redefining school readiness

When parents think about school readiness, they often think about academic milestones: recognizing letters, counting, writing their name. These matter — but they're only part of the picture.

**A truly school-ready child is one who can walk into a classroom and feel capable.**

## The pillars of school readiness

### 1. Regulation

Can the child manage transitions, big feelings, and mild frustration without dysregulating? This is the foundation of every other school skill.

### 2. Attention

Not just the ability to sit still — but to attend to a teacher, a story, a task, and return to it after a distraction.

### 3. Peer skills

Sharing, turn-taking, reading social cues, joining play. These are the quiet skills that shape a child's school experience.

### 4. Communication

Expressing needs, asking for help, telling a story, following instructions. Language is the bridge between a child and their classroom.

### 5. Self-care

Putting on shoes, opening a lunchbox, using the bathroom independently. Small things that add up to big confidence.

### 6. Pre-academic foundations

Letter and number recognition, pre-writing, early problem-solving. These are the visible part of readiness — but they rest on everything above.
