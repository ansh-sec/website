## A truth about therapy

The best therapy in the world only works for about an hour at a time. The other 167 hours of the week, it's the parent who is the therapist.

That's not pressure — it's a design principle.

## What parent coaching actually is

Parent coaching is not parenting advice. It's a clinical skill transfer. It's us handing you the same tools we use in session, adapted for your home, your schedule, and your child's specific profile.

## What it changes

### 1. Consistency

Children thrive on consistency. When parents carry therapeutic strategies into daily life, progress accelerates dramatically.

### 2. Confidence

Most parents feel unsure when their child is struggling. Coaching replaces uncertainty with a clear, doable plan.

### 3. Relationship

Therapy that excludes parents often accidentally creates a wedge — the child learns to be different with the therapist than with the parent. Coaching closes that gap.

### 4. Sustainability

The families who sustain progress are the ones who understand the *why* behind the *what*.

## How we do it

Every program at JOT Wellness includes parent coaching as a core component — not an add-on. This might look like:

- Brief coaching at the end of every session
- Dedicated monthly parent sessions
- Written home plans
- WhatsApp check-ins for real-life questions

## A gentle note

If you've ever left a therapy session wondering *what do I actually do when I get home?* — that's not your failure. It's a sign the therapy model is incomplete.

Good therapy makes the parent a co-therapist, not a spectator.
