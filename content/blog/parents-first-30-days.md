## First, breathe

If you've just received a diagnosis for your child — ASD, ADHD, a speech delay, a learning difference — the first thing to know is this: **you are not behind**. You're at the beginning of a journey, and you're not expected to have the map yet.

## Week 1: Pause and process

Resist the urge to immediately research everything, book every therapy, and fix everything at once. The first week is for emotional processing. Let the diagnosis settle. Talk to your partner. Call a trusted friend.

## Week 2: Find a trusted guide

You don't need to understand everything — you just need one trusted person to help you understand. That might be a developmental pediatrician, a clinical psychologist, or a therapy team. Ask questions. Take notes. Trust your gut.

## Week 3: Understand your child's profile

A diagnosis is a label. Your child is a profile. Ask your guide to help you understand:

- Strengths
- Specific areas of need
- Which therapies are most relevant right now
- What to prioritize first

## Week 4: Start small

Pick **one or two** therapy areas to start with. You can always add more later. The families who thrive are the ones who pace themselves, not the ones who sprint.

## A few truths to hold

- Diagnosis doesn't change your child — it changes your understanding of them.
- Progress is non-linear. Small shifts compound.
- You don't need to be an expert. You need to be a loving, consistent parent.
- Asking for help is not failure — it's the beginning of real support.

> "You don't have to have the whole plan. You just have to take the next right step."
